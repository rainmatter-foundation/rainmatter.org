            Crossing seven of nine planetary boundaries is a stark reminder that we must act now, not later. It calls for building a resilient economy, and it cannot be done in silos.<br/><br/>
            This forms the basis of the report on "The State of Climate Response in India", a collaborative initiative by Deloitte India and the Rainmatter Foundation, which was launched on 30th October 2025.<br/><br/>
            The report consolidates:
            <ul>
                <li>Climate indicators to enable data-driven strategies, aligning growth and environmental stability.</li>
                <li>Interconnected risks that highlight the deeply systemic nature of climate stressors, with ripple effects across sectors.</li>
                <li>A nationwide survey of 1,700 households that examines how people understand and experience climate change.</li>
                <li>A corporate climate readiness survey which engaged more than 50 leading corporations across sectors.</li>
                <li>Case studies that show climate leadership can coexist with profitability.</li>
                <li>And a framework for action turning risk into resilience that calls for a whole-of-economy approach.</li>
            </ul>
            The study shows that by embracing interconnected complexity and coming out of silos, businesses and governments can unlock new opportunities for resilience and innovation.
